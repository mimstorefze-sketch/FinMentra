IFRS 15 — The 5-Step Revenue Model

Para 9, 22, 31, 47, 73, 105
Step 1: Identify the contract with a customer
Step 2: Identify performance obligations
Step 3: Determine the transaction price
Step 4: Allocate price to obligations
Step 5: Recognise revenue when each obligation is satisfied

Key test: Revenue = when CONTROL transfers, not when cash is received or invoice issued.

IAS 37 vs IAS 10
Common Confusion
IAS 37 PROVISIONS
Past event creates present obligation. Probable outflow. Reliable estimate. → RECOGNISE liability.
IAS 10 EVENTS
Adjusting: conditions existed at year-end. Non-adjusting: arose after. → Adjust vs disclose.

⚠️ The most commonly missed exam points. Master these to gain easy marks.
IFRS 15 — Revenue
❌ Revenue recognised when invoice is issued.
✅ Recognised when CONTROL transfers to customer.
❌ Upfront payment = immediate revenue.
✅ Creates a contract LIABILITY (Para 106).
IAS 36 — Impairment
❌ Goodwill impairment can be reversed.
✅ NEVER reversed under IFRS (IAS 36.124).
❌ Recoverable amount = fair value.
✅ RA = HIGHER of FVLCD and VIU.
IFRS 16 — Leases
❌ Operating leases still off-balance-sheet after 2019.
✅ IFRS 16 puts ALL lessee leases on SFP.
IAS 37 — Provisions
❌ Can provision for future operating losses.
✅ No present obligation → cannot recognise.
IAS 16 — PPE
❌ Staff training costs are capitalised with a new asset.
✅ Training costs are ALWAYS expensed immediately.

IFRS 15
Client: TechSoft Ltd, a SaaS company.

TechSoft signs a 3-year contract for $360,000 paid upfront on 1 Jan. The contract includes software access ($240,000) and annual support ($120,000). The software is delivered on day 1. How should TechSoft recognise revenue?
✅ CORRECT — IFRS 15 Para 84-86
Separate performance obligations: (1) Software licence — transferred at a point in time → $240k on day 1. (2) Annual support — performance over time → $40k/year.

⚖️ Professional ethics cases based on IESBA Code of Ethics and real exam scenarios.
Case 1 — Independence
Threat
Your audit firm has been asked to also prepare the financial statements for Nexus Corp, whose audit you lead. The fee would be significant.
What type of threat arises?
Self-review threat — reviewing your own firm's work creates a threat to objectivity. Safeguards: use a different team, or decline the preparation engagement.
Case 2 — Confidentiality
Dilemma
During an audit you discover strong evidence that your client has been evading tax. The client refuses to disclose this. What should you do?
The auditor faces a conflict between confidentiality (IESBA Para 114) and public interest. If law mandates reporting (e.g. POCA/SAR), they must report. Always seek legal advice first.

IAS 16 — Straight-Line Depreciation
Worked
Facts: PPE cost $50,000, residual value $5,000, useful life 10 years.

Depreciation = (Cost − Residual) / Useful Life
= ($50,000 − $5,000) / 10 = $4,500/year
Journal Entry (each year):
Dr Depreciation Expense $4,500
  Cr Accumulated Depreciation $4,500

NBV after Year 3: $50,000 − (3 × $4,500) = $36,500
IFRS 9 — ECL Calculation
Worked
Facts: Trade receivable $100,000, Stage 1 (12-month). PD = 3%, LGD = 45%.
ECL = PD × LGD × EAD
= 3% × 45% × $100,000 = $1,350
Journal Entry:
Dr Credit Loss Expense $1,350
  Cr Loss Allowance $1,350

If SICR occurs → move to Stage 2 → use Lifetime ECL instead of 12-month.
